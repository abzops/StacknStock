Product

One integrated system. Every component purpose-built.

How it works

Five steps from inbound to dispatch

The WES orchestrates every movement — no manual intervention needed beyond pick/pack at the workstation.

01

Inbound & putaway

Goods scanned at inbound. WES assigns optimal bin locations in real time.

02

Dynamic slotting

WES continuously reshuffles hot SKUs closer to the workstation.

03

Retrieval to station

Bots retrieve totes autonomously. Zero picker walking.

04

Pick / Pack

Operator picks from tote, packs and scans out at workstation.

05

Dispatch & analytics

Order dispatched. WES updates inventory and generates shift reports.

FAQ

Common questions

A single-cluster deployment (1–2 pods + 1 workstation) can fit in approximately 300–500 sq ft depending on ceiling height and configuration. Our team assesses your site and recommends the minimum viable layout during the survey phase.
System uptime targets are north of 95% for the ASRS hardware. The WES runs on redundant cloud/edge infrastructure. Actual uptime depends on power quality, maintenance adherence, and site conditions. Remote monitoring flags issues early.
No dedicated technician needed at most deployments. Preventive maintenance is scheduled quarterly. We train your in-house team for first-level maintenance. Critical issues are handled remotely or via onsite dispatch.
Yes. The WES exposes a REST API and supports webhook-based event streaming. We have integration blueprints for common platforms. Custom integrations are supported. Speak to our engineering team for specifics.
A single-cluster deployment typically requires a standard 3-phase connection. Power draw is low relative to throughput — bots are regenerative-braking capable. We recommend a UPS for the WES server rack. Exact specs provided during site survey.
The WES monitors bin levels in real time and generates replenishment tasks automatically when stock drops below thresholds. Operators are notified via the workstation display. The system can integrate with your procurement workflow to trigger supplier orders.
Stack n Stock is optimized for small-to-medium items (roughly up to 50×40×30 cm per tote). High-SKU count, moderate-to-high velocity environments are ideal. Very large or irregularly shaped items are not a fit. Our team can assess your SKU profile during pre-sales.
Indicatively 6–8 weeks from contract to go-live for a single-cluster deployment. Larger deployments may take 12–16 weeks. Timeline depends on site readiness, civil work, and permit approvals. Digital twin commissioning happens in parallel with manufacturing.
Three models: (1) CapEx purchase — you own the system. (2) OpEx lease — monthly payments, we maintain. (3) FaaS — per-order pricing, we operate the node. Pricing is configuration-dependent; contact us for a quote.
The WES can run in cloud or on-premises mode. Data is encrypted in transit and at rest. You retain ownership of your data. We do not sell or share operational data. An air-gapped deployment option is available for secure contexts.
