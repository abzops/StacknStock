SNS-SEC-A1 / Story

Why we
built this

Traditional warehousing is a capital trap. You build, you're stuck. Demand shifts, you can't move. Leases expire, you write off millions in bespoke fit-out. Stack n Stock was born from frustration with that model.

"Infrastructure should behave like the supply chains it serves: responsive, lean, and relocatable."

We engineer each pod at our manufacturing facility to ISO 668 standards, integrating power, data, racking, MHE, and automation before a single unit leaves the factory floor. Clients receive a live, operational system — not a construction project.

From a single staging pod for a manufacturer's shop floor to a multi-pod AS/RS hub serving thousands of e-commerce orders per day, the unit of deployment is always the same: the pod.

SNS-SEC-A2 / Engineering Logic

The 40-foot
container logic

SPEC-01

Universal Standard

ISO 668 Class 1C containers share identical corner-casting geometry across every port, rail yard, and truck chassis on earth. Our pods inherit this interoperability — they travel on standard equipment and fit standard cranes without special permits.

SPEC-02

Structural Integrity

A standard 40-ft container carries up to 30,480 kg and stacks 9-high. That structural headroom means we can install dense racking, conveyors, and AS/RS machinery without compromising pod integrity or requiring civil groundwork.

SPEC-03

Rapid Logistics

Because our pods conform to ISO container profiles, they can be moved via existing freight infrastructure — flatbed trucks, rail, and ship — without oversize load permits. Your warehouse follows your business wherever it grows.

SPEC-04

Modular Composition

40-ft pods interlock end-to-end or side-by-side. Two pods form an 80-ft aisle. Four pods form a 2×2 picking matrix with internal connecting openings. The geometry scales predictably — no custom engineering for each new layout.
